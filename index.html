Hemodynamic Monitoring from Bed-Side to MRI


Products

Blood flow and Stroke Volume are common clinical metric for monitoring of cardiovascular diseases but current measurement methods are costly or uncomfortable for patients, and lack compatibility with modern diagnostic imaging modalities used in hospitals such as MRI. We have developed a wearable technology to non-invasively provide hemodynamic monitoring, while maintaining CT and MRI-compatibility. Based on interactions initially observed during MR imaging, between the strong magnetic field and aortic blood flow, we utilize induced magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) voltages (VMHD) and known bio-potentials to accurately monitor cardiac activity and hemodynamics in real-time. This allows for the first patient monitor that can be used continuously from bed-side to MRI, while delivering high-fidelity cardiac information to overseeing clinicians.
